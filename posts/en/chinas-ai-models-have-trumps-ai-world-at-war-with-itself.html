Rising Competition from Chinese AI Models Triggers Infighting Among Trump’s Policy Advisors

www.technologyreview.com · Mon, 20 Jul 2026 18:00:00 +0000

Public disputes among top advisors to Donald Trump over how to counter competitive Chinese AI models have revealed deep strategic divides within the administration's technology policy team.

Recent breakthroughs in Chinese artificial intelligence capabilities have sparked intense debate in Washington, leading to unprecedented public friction among key policy advisors within President Donald Trump's inner circle. Over the weekend, prominent current and former artificial intelligence advisors engaged in heated exchanges, targeting leading American technology firms and prominent industry leaders. The internal discord noticeably escalated after David Sacks, designated as a leading figure on national AI and cryptocurrency policy, publicly criticized the strategic priorities of domestic tech giants, accusing them of failing to adequately address foreign competitive threats.

At the core of the disagreement lies a fundamental strategic dilemma regarding how the United States should counter China's rapid advancements in open-source and proprietary AI architectures. Certain factions within the administration advocate for aggressive national security restrictions, stricter export controls, and heightened regulatory oversight to curb foreign progress. Conversely, rival voices within the coalition argue that heavy-handed regulations will stifle American innovation, ultimately handing global technological leadership to Beijing. This bitter ideological divide has exposed deep fractures over whether to prioritize defensive security measures or uninhibited market expansion.

As low-cost Chinese AI models continue to demonstrate high performance on global benchmarks, the internal conflict underscores growing anxieties regarding long-term Western technological superiority. Industry analysts warn that without a clear and unified policy framework, Washington risks sending contradictory signals to private tech firms, investors, and international partners. The expanding rift among high-profile advisors highlights that establishing a coherent national strategy for artificial intelligence will serve as one of the most complex and contentious policy challenges facing the administration moving forward.
