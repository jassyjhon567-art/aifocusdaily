Is Apple One Still Value for Money After Recent Price Hikes?

9to5mac.com · Sat, 18 Jul 2026 21:38:18 +0000

Following Apple's recent price hikes for Apple Music and Apple One bundles, consumers must re-evaluate their digital usage to determine if the subscription packages still offer genuine value.

Tech giant Apple has officially announced price increases for its popular standalone music streaming service, Apple Music, alongside rate hikes for two of its comprehensive Apple One subscription bundles. This strategic decision marks a significant shift in Apple’s pricing structure as the company seeks to maximize revenue from its highly successful services division. For consumers who have grown accustomed to these digital offerings, the sudden price increase raises critical questions about the overall value of maintaining multiple individual subscriptions versus opting for a bundled package.

Currently, Apple boasts an extensive ecosystem of digital services designed to cater to various lifestyle and entertainment needs, including Apple TV+, Apple Music, Apple Arcade, Apple Fitness+, News+, and iCloud storage. The Apple One bundle was originally introduced as a cost-effective solution, allowing users to combine these diverse platforms into a single, discounted monthly subscription. However, with the latest price adjustments affecting both individual plans and family bundles, subscribers must now carefully calculate whether they are actually utilizing enough of these services to justify the higher recurring monthly cost.

Ultimately, deciding whether Apple One remains worth the investment depends heavily on your household's individual usage patterns. For power users who actively stream music, watch original television content, play mobile games, and require extra cloud storage, the bundled option still offers noticeable savings compared to purchasing each service separately. Conversely, for casual users who only rely on one or two specific services, it might be far more cost-effective to cancel the bundle and subscribe only to what they actually need. Conducting a thorough assessment of your digital habits is now essential to avoid unnecessary subscription creep.
