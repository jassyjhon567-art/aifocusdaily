Astronomers Discover First Atmosphere on a Rocky Planet in the Habitable Zone

www.sciencedaily.com · Tue, 21 Jul 2026 06:57:41 EDT

Astronomers have confirmed the first atmosphere around a rocky exoplanet in a habitable zone, LHS 1140 b, detected through leaking helium gas 48 light-years away.

In a groundbreaking astronomical discovery, scientists have identified the first confirmed atmosphere surrounding a rocky exoplanet located within its host star's habitable zone. The distant terrestrial world, designated as LHS 1140 b, is situated approximately 48 light-years away from Earth in the constellation Cetus. This major milestone in exoplanetary science provides researchers with an unprecedented opportunity to study a rocky world outside our solar system that orbits at an ideal distance where surface temperatures might allow liquid water to exist.

The presence of this atmospheric layer was detected by observing helium gas slowly leaking into space from the planet's upper atmospheric region. LHS 1140 b orbits a cool, low-mass red dwarf star, an astronomical environment where intense stellar radiation typically strips away the fragile gaseous layers of nearby planets. Surprisingly, current observational data suggests that this planet has managed to preserve its gaseous envelope for billions of years, defying conventional theoretical models regarding planetary atmospheric evolution around active stars.

The extraordinary preservation of an atmospheric envelope around a rocky planet in a habitable orbit immediately elevates LHS 1140 b to one of the highest-priority targets for future astronomical investigations. Scientists plan to utilize powerful next-generation space telescopes to conduct comprehensive spectroscopic studies to analyze the precise chemical composition of its atmosphere. Uncovering signatures of key gases could provide crucial insights into its climate structure, bringing humanity closer to evaluating the true habitability of planets beyond our solar system.
