Apple Store App Likely to Introduce AI-Powered Virtual Shopping Assistant

9to5mac.com · Wed, 22 Jul 2026 20:56:34 +0000

Apple has updated its Apple Store app privacy policy, signaling the imminent launch of an AI-powered virtual shopping assistant designed to help users browse products and receive personalized recommendations.

Apple appears to be preparing a major digital update for its retail ecosystem by integrating an artificial intelligence-powered virtual shopping assistant directly into the official Apple Store app. Recent discoveries reveal that the tech giant has quietly updated the application's privacy policy to include specific legal terms governing a yet-to-be-released virtual shopping tool. This strategic update strongly suggests that Apple is actively laying the necessary operational and regulatory groundwork to launch an intelligent conversational feature aimed at enhancing how consumers browse and purchase products.

According to the newly added privacy policy clauses, the upcoming virtual assistant is expected to help users navigate Apple's extensive product offerings, answer specific technical queries, and offer personalized product recommendations. While Apple has not yet formally announced the feature through an official press event, the updated policy language indicates that user interaction data may be processed securely to deliver tailored suggestions and improve customer service efficiency. This move directly aligns with Apple's broader strategy of integrating advanced artificial intelligence and machine learning capabilities into its software services.

As online retail experiences increasingly rely on generative artificial intelligence, Apple's implementation could significantly streamline how customers buy hardware, trade in old devices, and select accessories. Industry observers anticipate that the virtual assistant will seamlessly connect with individual user accounts, enabling instant device compatibility checks, customized recommendations, and real-time order support. Although an official rollout schedule remains unconfirmed by the company, the privacy policy modification strongly hints that a public launch could happen in the near future.
