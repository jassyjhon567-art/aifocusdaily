FBI Issues Alert Over Deepfake Videos Impersonating Internet Crime Complaint Center Leadership

www.infosecurity-magazine.com · Tue, 21 Jul 2026 12:00:00 GMT

The FBI has issued a public warning regarding AI-generated deepfake videos impersonating IC3 leadership to lure users to malicious, spoofed websites.

The Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) has issued an urgent public warning regarding sophisticated deepfake videos that impersonate leadership from the Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3). According to federal officials, cybercriminals are increasingly leveraging advanced artificial intelligence tools to create highly realistic video recordings of agency officials. These manipulative clips are engineered to deceive victims of previous cyber scams into believing they are receiving direct assistance from high-ranking law enforcement authorities.

In these fraudulent videos, synthetic versions of IC3 leaders direct unsuspecting viewers to spoofed complaint portals and malicious websites. Once users land on these illegitimate domains, they are prompted to enter sensitive personal information, banking details, or account credentials under the pretense of recovering stolen funds or submitting official incident reports. Threat actors then harvest this data to orchestrate secondary extortion schemes, commit identity theft, or drain financial accounts, further exploiting individuals who are already vulnerable.

To protect against these evolving threats, the FBI advises the public to carefully verify web domain URLs and rely solely on official government portals ending in .gov, such as the authentic ic3.gov website. Federal authorities emphasized that legitimate agency representatives will never demand payments, request sensitive financial credentials, or direct individuals to unverified third-party platforms via unsolicited social media posts or synthetic video broadcasts. Anyone who suspects they have encountered a fake video or fallen victim to a spoofed domain is strongly encouraged to report the incident immediately through official, verified government channels.
