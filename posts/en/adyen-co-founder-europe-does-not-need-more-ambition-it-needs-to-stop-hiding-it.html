Europe Must Stop Hiding Its Tech Ambitions, Says Adyen Co-Founder

fortune.com · Thu, 23 Jul 2026 07:00:00 +0000

Adyen co-founder Thijn Lamers argues that European tech startups must overcome cultural humility and boldly showcase their ambitions to compete effectively with global market leaders.

European technology companies do not suffer from inferior products or a lack of fundamental talent, but rather from a cultural hesitation to project their true ambitions. According to Thijn Lamers, a co-founder of the global payments giant Adyen, the continent's tech ecosystem is consistently held back by a deeply ingrained sense of modesty. Rather than struggling with innovation, European entrepreneurs frequently mask their broader market potential, which ultimately prevents promising local startups from expanding into world-leading financial and technological enterprises.

Lamers emphasizes that while European founders possess the technical capabilities and long-term vision required to build transformative global platforms, their tendency to understate achievements creates a distinct disadvantage. In stark contrast to the bold narrative-building and aggressive risk-taking characteristic of Silicon Valley, European corporate culture often favors quiet, incremental progress. This restraint can significantly limit access to large-scale venture funding, diminish global brand visibility, and make it harder to attract top-tier international talent drawn to ambitious tech leaders.

To successfully foster the next wave of global technology titans, Lamers argues that European founders, venture capitalists, and policymakers must fundamentally shift their mindset and embrace their capabilities more openly. By abandoning self-imposed humility and actively promoting technological breakthroughs on the global stage, Europe can build an environment capable of standing toe-to-toe with major international tech hubs. Championing this unreserved ambition remains essential if the region hopes to cultivate more high-value success stories on the scale of Adyen in the coming decade.
