Unlocking History: Alan Turing's Secret 'Delilah' Voice Encryption Papers Sold at Auction

www.schneier.com · 2026-07-17T11:02:21Z

A rare archive of Alan Turing's wartime papers detailing 'Delilah,' his secret portable voice-encryption system, was auctioned in London for nearly half a million dollars.

In November 2023, a major piece of cryptographic history was unveiled to the public when a large cache of Alan Turing's wartime papers was successfully auctioned in London for nearly half a million U.S. dollars. Nicknamed the 'Bayley papers,' this previously unknown archive offers an unprecedented and deeply personal glimpse into the legendary mathematician's mind. The collection contains numerous sheets written in Turing's own handwriting, detailing his highly classified engineering work during the critical final years of the Second World War.

At the heart of these newly discovered documents is the 'Delilah' project, a top-secret portable voice-encryption system developed by Turing between 1943 and 1945. Named after the biblical figure famous for her deceptive nature, the Delilah system was designed to secure military voice communications against enemy interception. Alongside Turing's own sketches and explanations, the papers contain extensive technical notes recorded by Donald Bayley, who worked closely as Turing's assistant and meticulously captured his spoken ideas in real-time.

The historical survival of these invaluable documents is entirely due to Bayley, who quietly preserved them for decades until his own passing in 2020, outliving Turing by some 66 years. By keeping these records safe, Bayley protected a crucial chapter of global technological history that might have otherwise been lost forever. This landmark auction not only underscores the enduring fascination with Turing's cryptographic genius but also provides modern researchers with vital new insights into early voice security.
